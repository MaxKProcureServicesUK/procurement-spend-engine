Narrative — five executive questions
1 — Where should we look first?
Data is structurally sound — analysis can proceed on the operating spend base (£12.5M) immediately. The construction and leasehold spend (£12.3M) is contractually committed and outside the addressable base for standard procurement levers. Priority action: obtain the Andway Healthcare rate card (PSI-A) to convert a detected price variance signal into a quantified contract review. In parallel, validate agency staff concentration at King's Court and Heron Lodge (PSI-E) against bed occupancy and construction schedules before any procurement action.
2 — Why does it matter?
The operating spend base is where procurement can act. Five of six PSL Insights are active or conditionally active. Two enrichment datasets — the contract register and prior year spend — would materially increase the precision and confidence of every insight. Without the contract register, price variance can be identified but not quantified against contracted rates.
5 — What next, in what order?
1. Obtain Andway Healthcare rate card (1 hour). 2. Validate KIN/HER agency spend against bed occupancy (1 week with Operations). 3. Agree consolidated delivery schedule for care supplies and food (2–4 weeks with Operations). 4. Brakes Bros group account confirmation (1 day). 5. Contract register and prior year spend extract for full PSL Insights confidence (Finance, 1–2 days each).

Each dataset unlocks specific PSL Insights and increases confidence. Core engine logic does not change. Datasets are incorporated by PSL analysts into each client's bespoke engine instance — this is not a self-service upload interface.
Analyst-operated model: Enrichment datasets are sourced, validated, and loaded by the PSL analyst as part of the client engagement workflow.
