Three structural findings from 307,818 invoice lines
Finding 1 — Facility & Property Management dominates at 50.1% (£12.4M). This PSL Procurement Category captures Construction, Leasehold, and Real Estate — largely contractual commitments. The actionable procurement base is the remaining £12.4M across Corporate & Admin Support, ICT, Logistics, and Energy & Utilities.

Finding 2 — 63.1% of spend has no purchase order (£15.6M), but genuine operational maverick is ~£1.2M. The majority of no-PO spend is in Facility & Property Management categories (Construction, Leasehold, Utilities) which are legitimately off-PO. Transport (100% no-PO, £531K) and Repair & Maintenance (78% no-PO, £684K) are the genuine commercial control failures.

Finding 3 — Transport (SIC H, PSL Logistics & Distribution) carries 0.71 tCO₂e per £1,000 — 4.4× the portfolio average. SUP-009 generates 336 tonnes CO₂e from just £447K spend: 8.5% of total carbon from 1.8% of spend. This is the most acute carbon/spend divergence in the dataset and the clearest single decarbonisation target.

Why SIC → PSL Procurement Category matters
The hierarchy Invoice → Supplier → SIC → PSL Procurement Category → PSL Insight Lens is the engine of SPIE. SIC classification is the technical step — it maps each supplier to a UK-standard industry code with an associated carbon intensity ratio. The PSL Procurement Category is the commercial step — it translates SIC codes into the seven procurement-meaningful groupings that PSL analysts use: Facility & Property Management, Corporate & Admin Support, ICT, Energy & Utilities, Logistics & Distribution, Banking/Finance/Insurance, and Other.

This two-step classification means every invoice line carries both a regulatory/technical identity (SIC) and a commercial procurement identity (PSL Category) — enabling consistent analysis across clients regardless of how their own internal categories are named.

SPIE v2.0 — product summary
SPIE (Spend Procurement Intelligence Engine) is a PSL Intelligence Engine. It transforms raw supplier invoice data into structured commercial and carbon intelligence using a five-layer hierarchy: Invoice → Supplier → SIC Section → PSL Procurement Category → PSL Insight Lens.

The 6 PSL Insight Lenses (Supplier Rationalisation, Pricing Variance, Demand Management, Maverick Spend, Category Strategy, Sustainability & Carbon) are the analytical core — the productised form of PSL's 25-year procurement diagnostic methodology.

SIC classification is the technical enhancement layer: it gives every supplier a carbon intensity ratio, a market classification, and a Companies House-validated identity. PSL Procurement Categories are the commercial layer: seven groupings (Facility & Property Management, Corporate & Admin Support, ICT, Energy & Utilities, Logistics & Distribution, Banking/Finance/Insurance, Other) that make SIC-based intelligence legible to procurement and finance decision-makers.

Target market: UK organisations with £25M–£250M annual turnover. Time from data receipt to first intelligence output: 72 hours. No ERP integration required.
DISCLAIMER: All data is fully anonymised. Client A = anonymised client. SUP-001+ = anonymised supplier names. Site codes = anonymised site names. Carbon estimates are indicative, derived from UK SIC-based intensity ratios (DEFRA/GHG Protocol methodology) — not a verified carbon audit. Saving estimates are heuristic-based and require individual commercial validation. Prepared by PSL. © PSL — all analytical methodology is PSL IP.
